Project Brief

Tired of posting on confession walls with no replies? Disappointed by missed chances for room subleases?

Don't let opportunities slip away again! The Magnet App is here to help: sharing event info, connecting you with others for carpooling, subleasing, and many more.

Check out the High-Fidelity Prototype here.

Project Goal

- Bridge users seeking connections with one another
- Increase the success rate of finding a buddy (e.g. for carpool, concerts, etc.)
- Develop a platform that delivers real-time event updates

Target Users
Research Summary


1. Only 3% of people are satisfied with their current method of connecting with others
2. More than 33% of people have missed an event for more than 5 times
3. Posting on social media is the most common way to finding or connect with people

Main Frustrations

Based on the survey and user interviews, the top 3 frustrations of current networking methods are embarrassments, inefficiencies and low successful rates. Overall, these disappointments indicate that there are potential improvements that can be made to the current networking methods.

Main Features

#1. Personal Preference Setup

Providing users with the ability to create personalized profiles in order to receive targeted contents on their account.

Users can customize their preferences by selecting preferred categories in the 'My Preferences' page or by inputting their own category choices.

#2.The Calendar Features

The calendar helps organize and manage upcoming and saved events.

This calendar uses color-coding:
blue -> planned events
yellow -> saved events

Event times are also displayed in a list, allowing users to easily identify conflicts and adjust their schedule.

#3. Chat Box: Connect with Publishers

Users can easily connect with post publishers by clicking the "I am in!" button.

They can also leave messages for publishers, with the option to include the link of the post they just viewed. The link will automatically appear above the typing space, to help the publisher understand their inquiry better.

Design Challenges

Magnet was formerly known as Not Alone, but I changed it to Magnet as as research and feedback from users revealed that the previous name, Not Alone, sounded more like a dating app. I also conducted a survey to gather opinions on the names "Buoy" and "Beacon". According to the survey results, more than 70% of participants perceive "Buoy" as a dating application for men, while over 60% of respondents associate "Beacon" with an investment banking app.

I've been racking my brain for a while trying to think of the perfect name for this app, and I finally landed on "Magnet" because I believe:

To connect with a person or a group of people, you can position yourself as a "magnet" in your circle by consistantly making yourself approachable through networking events, online communities, etc.

This is the idea behind the name "Magnet" for this application: to build bridges between people and bring people together.

Learnings and Takeaways

This was my first project as a solo designer, where I conducted thorough user research and analysis to gain a deeper understanding of my users.

I spent a considerable amount of time interviewing users and gathering feedback to understand their needs and preferences. I was surprised to find that each user had a unique perspective and approach to networking.

I also studied colour psychology in order to find the most fitting colour scheme for the application. This was a challenging task, as colours can evoke different emotions and associations in different people. As you might be aware of, I had chosen purple as the primary colour previously, but later realized it gave the impression of a beauty product application, which was not the desired outcome. I eventually settled on a colour scheme that I felt was more appropriate for the networking platform and that would resonate with users.

Please let me know if you have any suggestions or critiques, I'm open to hear them!
